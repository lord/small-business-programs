Emergency Assistance Eligibility Wizard

The COVID-19 outbreak is causing extraordinary disruption for companies and non-profits across the United States. The NJEDA wants to make it easy for organizations to understand what support programs are available to you to help stabilize your operations and get back on a pathway to growth.

This is a beta version of the Eligibility Wizard that focuses on the Federal PPP and SBA disaster loan programs. We will be updating it regularly as new federal, state, local, and philanthropic programs become available.

To apply for a program listed below, you need to apply through the appropriate administering agency. The administering agency will determine all eligibility requirements after you submit an application.

Federal Small Business Administration (SBA) Emergency Injury Disaster Loans (EIDL)

Description: Provides low-interest federal disaster loans for working capital to small businesses suffering substantial economic injury as a result of the Coronavirus (COVID-19).

Eligible Uses: Working capital

Availability: Loans are administered by the federal government. Businesses are advised to submit applications as soon as possible.

Learn more here: https://disasterloan.sba.gov/ela

Federal Economic Injury Disaster Loan Advance

Description: Provides emergency advance of up to $10,000 to small businesses and private non-profits harmed by COVID-19 within 3 days of applying for an SBA Economic Injury Disaster Loan (EIDL). This loan advance will not have to be repaid.

Eligible Uses: May be used to keep employees on payroll, to pay for sick leave, meet increased production costs due to supply chain disruptions, or pay business obligations, including debts, rent and mortgage payments.

Availability: Loans are administered by the federal government. Businesses must first apply for an EIDL.

Learn more here: https://www.sba.gov/page/coronavirus-covid-19-small-business-guidance-loan-resources

Paycheck Protection Program (PPP) Loans

Description: Provides cash-flow assistance through 100 percent federally guaranteed loans to employers who maintain their payroll during this emergency. If employers maintain their payroll, the loans would be forgiven.

Eligible Uses: May be used to keep employees on payroll, pay for vacation, parental, family, medical or sick leave, pay for dismissal or separation, pay for group health care benefits, retirement benefits, or pay for State or local tax assessed on the compensation of employees.

Availability: Loans are administered by eligible SBA 7(a) lenders. SBA has a free referral service tool called Lender Match to help find a lender near you.

Learn more here: https://www.sba.gov/page/coronavirus-covid-19-small-business-guidance-loan-resources

Federal 7(a) Loan

Description: Federal Small Business Administration works with lenders to provide up to $5 million to small businesses. The SBA will guarantee up to 85 percent of loans of $150,000 or less and 75 percent of loans of $150,000 or above.

Eligible Uses: Loans may be used to expand, acquire, or start a business, purchase or construct real estate, refinance existing business debt, buy equipment, provide working capital, purchase inventory.

Availability: The specific terms of the SBA loans are negotiated between the borrower and the participating lender.

Learn more here: https://www.sba.gov/partners/lenders/7a-loan-program

Small Business Debt Relief Program

Description: Provides immediate relief to small businesses with non-disaster SBA loans, in particular 7(a), 504, and microloans. Under it, SBA will cover all loan payments on these SBA loans, including principal, interest, and fees, for six months. This relief will also be available to new borrowers who take out loans within six months of the President signing the bill into law.

Eligible Uses: Relief from non-disaster SBA loans.

Availability: Relief is available immediately to those who hold a non-disaster SBA loan and to those who take out a 7(a), 504, or microloan within six months of the President signing the bill into law.
